Acoustic Scene Classification with `Imagined' Images


Yang Liu Eric Sommerlade Alexandros Neophytou Sunando Sengupta


True image  (only for reference)
Input waveforms
Imagined images reconstructed from audio recording

We consider the task of the acoustic scene classification with the `imagined' images reconstructed from a short audio recording. We show several results of our method on Youtube 8M dataset. Our model takes only an audio recording as input (the true image are shown just for reference). Note that our goal is not to reconstruct an accurate image of the scene, but rather to use these reconstructed images to classify the sound recording.


Abstract

Acoustic scene classification is a challenging task due due to the fact that the sound is sensitive to kind of environment, recording time, equipment used for capture and susceptible to contamination from sounds produced by other objects in the scene vicinity. On the contrary, it is an easy task for humans to recognise a scene from a short audio recording based on their knowledge and experience. In this paper, we introduce an audio-visual generative adversarial network (AVGAN) to classify the acoustic scene and reconstruct visual scenes from the sound. The proposed AVGAN is trained on a large number of audio-visual pairs of Youtube 8M. In the training step, visual features are generated by passing frames through an encoder network. Audio information is used to generate the `imagined' visual features with a generator network. These two features are distinguished by an additional discriminator. With the `imagined' feature, an extra decoder is used to reconstruct the `imagined' images, and a classifier gives the category of the sound. We test our purposed AVGAN on the scene classification task of Dcase 2019 and ESC-50 and get the highest accuracy compared to the baseline methods.
 

This figure describes the layers of the model we have used. The audio-visual pairs of the Youtube 8M dataset are used to train the encoder, decoder, generator and discriminator. The goal of using video dataset is to reconstruct the image from the audio recording. The 'real' feature and 'imagined' features are shown as blue boxes. The audio recording of Dcase dataset is used to train the classifier for predicting the category of the audio recording when the generator is fixed.
